Robotics

What

Robotics is a technological field that provides products that reduce man-hours required to perform a task, that reduce human risk in hazardous environments, and that reduces economic cost per task but currently provides a lower level of reliability than alternatives. The proper working of robotic systems often involves sensors, embedded processors, actuators, and communication devices for reactive control and/or data analysis.

Why

Exploration of areas with known or unknown hazards is made possible with robotic systems. Human knowledge itself is advanced through the exploration of our world as well as our solar system and beyond. This is a noble pursuit. Robots are becoming an increasingly important aspect of exploration as well as the related search and rescue field.

How

Most innovative advances in robotics today are performed by Academia. Professors with government and industry clients produce products and/or test theories. Robotic startups contribute to the innovation in the field but have more difficulty acquiring funding and are made up of small groups in localized areas.

Examples

  1. NASA's Mars Curiosity Rover is set to look for signs of alien life on Mars over a 2 year period.
  2. Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution's Center for Marine Robotics continues to support robotics entrepreneurship for innovation in marine operations.
  3. The University of Madrid recently produced an unmanned ground vehicle that is in the shape of a sphere with the intended use for crop surveys.
  4. The University of Nevada is conducting research in autonomous exploration and inspection using flying robots.
